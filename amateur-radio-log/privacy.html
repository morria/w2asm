Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 8, 2026

Amateur Radio Log ("the App") is designed with your privacy as a priority. This policy explains what data the App collects, how it is used, and your choices.

Data Collection

The App does not collect, transmit, or share any personal data with us or any third party. We have no servers, no analytics, and no tracking.

Data Storage

All data you enter into the App (QSO logs, callsigns, notes, settings) is stored:

  • Locally on your device using Apple's SwiftData framework.
  • In your personal iCloud account (if iCloud is enabled) for syncing across your devices. This data is stored in your private iCloud container and is not accessible to us or anyone else.

Your iCloud data is protected by Apple's encryption and security practices. For details, see Apple's iCloud security overview.

Third-Party Services

The App can optionally connect to the following third-party services only when you choose to and provide your own credentials:

  • QRZ.com — for callsign lookups and logbook synchronization. Your QRZ username and password are stored in your device's Keychain and sent directly to QRZ.com's API. We never see these credentials.
  • HamQTH.com — for callsign lookups. Same as above.
  • ARRL Logbook of the World (LoTW) — for downloading QSL confirmations. Same as above.

We are not affiliated with QRZ.com, HamQTH.com, or the ARRL. Please review their respective privacy policies for how they handle your data.

Credentials

Service credentials (usernames and passwords for QRZ, HamQTH, LoTW) are stored in Apple's Keychain, which provides hardware-backed encryption. Credentials are never stored in plain text, never transmitted to us, and are only sent to the respective service when you initiate a sync or lookup.

ADIF Files

When you import or export ADIF files, the App reads from or writes to files you select on your device. These files remain on your device (or in your iCloud Drive if you save them there). We never access these files.

Children's Privacy

The App does not knowingly collect any information from children under the age of 13.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be posted on this page with an updated "Last updated" date.

Contact

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy, please open an issue on our GitHub repository.
